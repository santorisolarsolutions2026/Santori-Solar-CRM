1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
1.1 What Is SolarCRM?
SolarCRM is a custom web-based Customer Relationship Management (CRM) application built specifically for a Solar Energy company's sales operations. It manages the complete end-to-end journey of a customer lead — from the moment a phone number is received, through multiple calling attempts, follow-ups, meeting booking, physical site visits, sales closure, order documentation, and final handoff to the operations team for installation.
The system replaces manual processes (WhatsApp groups, Excel sheets, verbal handoffs) with a structured, role-based digital pipeline. Every action is logged, every status is tracked, and every team member sees only what they are authorized to see.

1.4 Deployment Requirements
The app must be accessible from both desktop browsers and mobile browsers (responsive design).
Recommended deployment: VPS (DigitalOcean / AWS EC2) with Nginx reverse proxy.
SSL (HTTPS) is mandatory — file uploads and user credentials must be encrypted.
All uploaded documents must be stored in a private bucket (not publicly accessible by URL guessing).
Minimum server spec: 2 vCPU, 4 GB RAM, 50 GB SSD.
2. USER ROLES & PERMISSIONS
2.1 Complete Role Definitions
The system has 8 distinct roles. Each role has a specific dashboard view, specific permissions, and specific data visibility rules. The role is assigned at user creation time by the Admin and is stored in the users table.
Role 1 — Admin (Deepak Sir)
The supreme controller of the entire system.
Can create, edit, deactivate any user account across all roles.
Can view ALL leads regardless of assignment.
Can add, edit, delete, reassign any lead at any stage.
Can view all reports: company-wide, per team, per consultant, per stage.
Has access to the Live Link (real-time war-room view).
Can configure system settings: lead import, bulk upload, notification settings.
Only role with access to the Finance section in full.
Only one Admin account exists (Deepak Sir). This role should not be assignable to multiple people unless explicitly requested.
Role 2 — Sales Head
Second-in-command. Oversight across all managers and teams.
Can view all leads across all managers (same as Admin for visibility).
Can add new leads and assign them to any Manager.
Can edit any lead. Cannot delete leads.
Has access to company-wide performance reports.
Has access to the Live Link view.
Cannot access Finance section (order punching financials).
Cannot manage user accounts (add/edit/delete users).
Role 3 — Manager
Responsible for a specific group of leads assigned to them.
Can add new leads (these get assigned to their pool by default).
Can edit any lead within their own assigned pool.
Can assign Team Leaders (TL) and Consultants to leads within their pool.
Can view reports for their own team only (not other managers' teams).
Can add/edit/remove Team Leaders and Consultants within their team.
Cannot delete leads. Cannot access Finance. Cannot see other managers' leads.
Role 4 — Team Leader (TL)
Senior consultant who supervises a small group of Consultants.
Can add new leads (assigned to their team pool).
Can edit any lead assigned to their team.
Can assign Consultants within their team to leads (cannot assign TLs).
Can view performance reports for their own team members only.
Cannot manage user accounts. Cannot access Finance.
Cannot see leads outside their assigned team pool.
Role 5 — Consultant
Front-line salesperson. Handles lead nurturing through calling and meeting stages.
Can ONLY see leads explicitly assigned to them.
Can edit lead status, add remarks, update call logs on their assigned leads.
Can fill in the Meeting Booking Form (Stage 8) for their assigned leads.
Can view their own performance metrics only.
Cannot add new leads to the system. Cannot assign leads to others. Cannot delete leads.
Role 6 — PSA (Pre-Sales Agent)
Specialised calling team. Handles only the initial calling stages.
Can only update call-related statuses: DNP (Stage 2), Follow Up (Stage 3), Not Interested (Stage 4), Call Later (Stage 5), Call Disconnected (Stage 10), Switch Off (Stage 11).
Can add call remarks to their assigned leads.
CANNOT change a lead to Stage 8 (Meeting Booked) — that is the Consultant's job.
CANNOT view lead addresses, financial info, or meeting details.
Has their own supervisor: PSA TL → PSM (within PSA hierarchy).
Role 7 — Finance Team
Activated only after a lead reaches Stage 13 (Sale Done).
Can view and fill the Order Punching Form for Stage 13 leads.
Can upload and verify documents (Aadhaar, PAN, Electricity Bill, Bank Passbook).
Can view order status and payment information.
CANNOT see leads below Stage 13. CANNOT edit lead status or add call remarks.
CANNOT manage users or view sales pipeline.
Role 8 — Operations Team
Handles installation and post-sale work.
Sees only leads/orders that have been fully processed by Finance (after Stage 13, post order-punching).
Can update installation status on their assigned orders.
CANNOT see leads below Stage 13. CANNOT access financial or payment information.

3. LEAD PIPELINE & ALL 14 STAGES
3.1 Pipeline Overview
Every lead in the system has a "status" field that represents its current stage in the sales pipeline. The status can only be updated by authorized roles. When a status changes, the system must: (1) log the change with timestamp and user ID, (2) trigger any associated form (Meeting Booked, Order Punching), (3) send notifications to relevant team members, and (4) update dashboard counters.
3.2 All 14 Lead Statuses — Detailed Specification
Stage 1 — Fresh Lead (New Lead)
Trigger: Lead is created in the system by Manager or Admin.
Default status when any lead is added to the system.
No calls have been made yet.
Assigned Consultant can see this lead in their pipeline.
No additional form required. All fields from Lead Creation Form (Form A) are already filled.
System should show "Not Yet Called" badge in the UI.
Stage 2 — DNP (Did Not Pick Up)
Trigger: Consultant/PSA calls the customer and gets no answer.
Consultant changes status to DNP.
A mandatory Remark field must be filled: e.g., "Called at 11 AM, no answer."
System automatically logs: who changed it, when, and from which previous status.
No sub-form required.
The lead remains in active pipeline for follow-up.
UI badge: Orange/grey "DNP" label.
Stage 3 — Follow Up (Warm/Hot Lead)
Trigger: Customer was reached and showed some interest.
Consultant MUST specify sub-type in dropdown: "Warm" (mild interest) or "Hot" (strong interest, nearly ready to buy).
Consultant MUST fill a remark describing the conversation.
Consultant MUST set a "Follow Up Date & Time" — a scheduled callback. This creates a reminder.
The system must send a reminder notification to the Consultant 30 minutes before the follow-up time.
"Hot" leads should visually appear highlighted (e.g., yellow/gold border) in the lead list.
Stage 4 — Not Interested
Trigger: Customer explicitly says they do not want solar.
Lead is moved to an "inactive" or "dismissed" state.
Lead still appears in the system (not deleted) but does NOT appear in the active pipeline by default.
Consultant must add a remark: reason for disinterest.
Manager/Admin can reactivate a "Not Interested" lead if they choose.
Counts toward conversion rate denominator in reports.
Stage 5 — Call Later
Trigger: Customer asked to be called at a specific later time.
Consultant MUST set a "Call Back Date & Time" (date/time picker — required field).
System creates a scheduled reminder for the assigned Consultant.
Reminder fires at the scheduled time via in-app notification (and optionally push notification).
Remains in active pipeline.
Stage 6 — Already Installed
Trigger: Customer already has solar panels installed.
Lead is moved to "disqualified" state.
Does not appear in active pipeline. Appears in a separate "Disqualified" filter.
Consultant adds a remark confirming.
No further action required from sales team.
Stage 7 — Decision Pending for Meeting
Trigger: Customer is considering agreeing to a meeting but has not yet confirmed.
Consultant has pitched a meeting but customer is unsure.
Lead stays in active pipeline. A "Pending Meeting Decision" badge is shown.
Manager or TL should be able to see these leads filtered separately (high-value leads needing attention).
No sub-form required. Remark is mandatory.
Stage 8 — Meeting Booked
Trigger: Customer agrees to a physical site visit/meeting.
When Consultant changes status to Meeting Booked, a MANDATORY form (Form B) pops up immediately.
The status CANNOT be saved unless the Meeting Booking Form is completed in full.
Form B fields (all required unless noted):
Full Address (text)
Pin Code (6-digit number)
Mobile Number (pre-filled from lead, editable)
Alternate Mobile Number (optional)
Date of Meeting (date picker — must be today or future)
Time of Meeting (time picker — 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM)
Average Monthly Electricity Bill Amount in ₹ (number)
Connection Type (dropdown: Residential / Commercial)
After form submission: system sends notification to the Sales Executive / Meeting Team assigned to visit.
Lead appears in a "Meetings Booked" section on Manager dashboard.
Stage 9 — Meeting Already Done
Trigger: The physical site visit/meeting has taken place.
Status is changed by the Sales Executive (Consultant doing the meeting) after the visit.
Remark is mandatory: brief summary of meeting outcome.
This is a transitional stage — the lead should quickly move to Stage 14 (Meeting Ended) where the actual outcome is captured.
Stage 10 — Call Disconnected
Trigger: Customer answered but immediately hung up.
Remark is mandatory.
Remains in active pipeline for retry.
Functionally similar to DNP (Stage 2) but distinguishes deliberate hang-up from no-answer.
Stage 11 — Switch Off / Incoming Off
Trigger: Phone is switched off or incoming calls are blocked.
Remark is mandatory.
Remains in active pipeline.
After 5 consecutive Switch Off/DNP logs, system should flag the lead as "Unreachable" for Manager review.
Stage 12 — Can't Fit Solar (Infeasible)
Trigger: Physical inspection or discussion reveals the location is not suitable for solar installation.
Reasons include: too much shading, roof angle, structural issues, tenant property (not owned), etc.
Consultant must select a reason from dropdown: Shading Issue / Roof Structure / Rented Property / Landlord Refusal / Too Small / Other.
If "Other" is selected, a text field appears for the specific reason.
Lead is moved to "Disqualified" state.
Stage 13 — Sale Done ⭐ (Most Critical Stage)
Trigger: Customer confirms the purchase of solar panels.
This is the most important stage in the entire pipeline.
When status is changed to Sale Done, the ORDER PUNCHING FORM (Form D) is immediately unlocked and must be completed.
The Form D is accessible to the Consultant (who triggers it) and the Finance Team.
Order Punching Form D fields (see Section 4.4 for full detail).
All four documents must be uploaded before order can be submitted.
After Finance submits the order, the Operations Team is notified and the order appears in their queue.
Admin dashboard shows live count of "Sales Done Today" and "Sales Done This Month."
A congratulations notification is sent to the Consultant and their Manager.
Stage 14 — Meeting Ended (Post-Meeting Sub-Status)
Trigger: After Stage 9 (Meeting Done), the Sales Executive updates the outcome.
When Stage 14 is selected, a sub-dropdown (Form C) appears with three options:
a) Sale Done → Lead immediately moves to Stage 13. Order Punching Form is unlocked.
b) Follow Up → Lead moves back to Stage 3. Consultant must set a follow-up date/time.
c) Not Interested → Lead moves to Stage 4. Remark is mandatory.
This stage ensures every meeting has a documented outcome — no lead can be left in "Meeting Done" indefinitely.

5. SCREEN-BY-SCREEN APPLICATION SPECIFICATION
5.1 Login Screen
URL: /login (public, no auth required)
Username field (text input).
Password field (password input with show/hide toggle).
Login button.
On successful login: JWT token issued, stored in httpOnly cookie or localStorage. User redirected to their role-specific dashboard.
On failed login: "Invalid credentials" error shown after 3 failed attempts. After 5 failures: account locked for 15 minutes. Admin can manually unlock accounts.

6. LIVE LINK FEATURE (ADMIN WAR-ROOM VIEW)
6.1 Overview
The Live Link is a real-time monitoring dashboard exclusively for Admin (Deepak Sir) and Sales Head. It requires no page refresh — data updates automatically using WebSocket (Socket.io) or polling every 5-10 seconds. It is designed to give a "war room" view of the entire sales operation at any moment.
6.2 URL & Access
URL: /live (protected route — redirects to login if user is not Admin or Sales Head)
Can also be shared as a secure URL with a time-limited token for read-only viewing on a TV/monitor.
6.3 Sections of the Live Link Screen
Section A — Live Activity Feed (Left Column)
A real-time scrolling feed of the last 30 lead status changes across all teams.
Each entry shows: Time (HH:MM:SS), Consultant Name, Customer Name, Stage Changed From → To, Remark snippet.
New entries appear at the top with a brief highlight animation (yellow flash).
Colour-coded by stage (using same badge colours as main pipeline).
Section B — Stage Distribution (Center)
Live count of how many leads are in each of the 14 stages right now.
Displayed as a horizontal stacked bar or a numbered grid with counts.
Updates in real-time as statuses change.
Section C — Online Users (Right Column)
List of all consultants and team members currently logged into the system.
Green dot = online/active (has made an action in last 5 minutes).
Yellow dot = idle (logged in but no activity in last 5-30 minutes).
Grey dot = offline (not logged in or session expired).
Shows which lead each online consultant is currently viewing or editing.
Section D — Today's KPI Bar (Top)
Sales Done Today | Meetings Booked Today | New Leads Today | Active Consultants Online
Each counter increments in real-time as events happen.
6.4 Technical Implementation Notes
Use Socket.io on the server. Emit events on every lead status change: socket.emit("statusChange", payload).
Live Link page subscribes to the "statusChange" channel.
User presence: emit "heartbeat" every 60 seconds from each active session. Server tracks last-seen timestamp per user.
For sessions without Socket.io, fall back to polling: GET /api/live/feed every 10 seconds.
Performance: limit live feed to last 30 events. Archive older events to database only.

10. BUSINESS LOGIC & EDGE CASES
10.1 Duplicate Lead Prevention
Mobile number is the unique identifier for a lead. The system must check for duplicates on every new lead creation.
Check is case-insensitive and strips spaces/dashes from the number before comparison.
If duplicate found: show warning with existing lead ID, assigned consultant, and current status. Block creation.
Admin can override this by clicking "Create Anyway" — this creates a new lead and adds a system note linking to the duplicate.
10.2 Lead Reassignment Rules
When a Consultant is deactivated, all their active leads (not in terminal stages) must be automatically reassigned to their TL.
If the TL is also deactivated, leads go to the Manager.
Both the old and new assignee receive a notification.
The reassignment is logged in the lead activity log with reason "User Deactivated."
Admin can manually reassign any lead at any time without restriction.
10.3 Unreachable Lead Auto-Flagging
If a lead has 5 consecutive log entries where status = DNP (Stage 2) or Switch Off (Stage 11) or Call Disconnected (Stage 10), the system automatically sets a flag: is_unreachable = true.
The Manager and TL receive a notification.
The lead appears in a special "Unreachable" filter on the Manager dashboard.
A Manager can dismiss the flag with a remark (e.g., "Will try again next week").
10.4 Order Submission Guard
The Order Punching Form (Form D) Submit button is HARD-DISABLED in the backend, not just the frontend.
The API endpoint POST /orders/:id/submit must check: count of uploaded documents with doc_types aadhaar, pan, electricity_bill, bank_passbook must all be present. If any is missing, return HTTP 422 with error.
Frontend validation is also required, but backend is the source of truth.
10.5 Status Change with Forms
Changing to Stage 8 (Meeting Booked): The API call to PATCH status must include all Form B fields in the same request body, or Form B must be submitted first (creating a meeting_bookings record) before the status change is accepted.
Recommended: combined endpoint POST /leads/:id/status that handles both in a single DB transaction.
If Form B data is missing when trying to set status = 8, return HTTP 422.
10.6 Data Visibility Enforcement (Backend)
ALL API responses must filter data based on role. Never rely on frontend filtering alone.
Consultant GET /leads must return ONLY leads where assigned_consultant_id = current user ID.
PSA GET /leads must return ONLY leads assigned to them, with mobile numbers masked (last 5 digits replaced with XXXXX).
Manager GET /leads must return ONLY leads where assigned_manager_id = current user ID.
Finance GET /orders must return ONLY orders in status: submitted | finance_verified | ops_assigned | completed.
Any attempt to access a lead/order outside the user's visibility scope must return HTTP 403 Forbidden.
10.7 Bulk Lead Import
Admin can upload an Excel (.xlsx) or CSV file to import leads in bulk.
Required columns in the file: customer_name, mobile, connection_type, sanctioned_load_kw, address, pin_code.
System validates each row: check for duplicates, validate phone format, validate required fields.
Import result: show a summary table — X leads imported successfully, Y leads skipped (with reasons: duplicate, invalid phone, missing required field).
Failed rows are available to download as a separate error report.
Maximum import size: 1,000 leads per file.

12. SECURITY & NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
12.1 Security Requirements
All passwords must be hashed using bcrypt (minimum 10 rounds). Plain-text passwords must never be stored.
JWT tokens must have an expiry of 24 hours. Refresh token mechanism recommended for mobile sessions.
All API endpoints must validate and sanitize input to prevent SQL injection and XSS.
File uploads must be validated: check MIME type server-side (not just extension). Only allow: image/jpeg, image/png, application/pdf.
Uploaded files must NOT be accessible via direct URL. All access must go through a signed URL endpoint with expiry.
Implement rate limiting: max 10 failed login attempts per IP per hour. Max 100 API requests per minute per user.
CORS must be configured to allow only the app's domain. No wildcard (*) in production.
Sensitive data (Aadhaar, PAN numbers) must not be logged in application logs.
12.2 Performance Requirements
Lead list page must load in under 2 seconds for up to 10,000 leads.
API response time must be under 500ms for 95th percentile requests.
Database indexes required on: leads.mobile, leads.status, leads.assigned_consultant_id, leads.assigned_manager_id, lead_activity_log.lead_id, notifications.user_id.
File uploads should be handled asynchronously — the API returns immediately with a job ID, and the client polls for upload status.
12.3 UI/UX Requirements
The application must be fully responsive and usable on mobile browsers (375px+ width).
Consultants will primarily use the app on mobile phones — the Lead Detail page and status change workflow must be mobile-first.
All tables must be horizontally scrollable on mobile.
Loading states must be shown for all async operations (spinners, skeleton loaders).
All destructive actions (delete lead, deactivate user) must have a confirmation dialog.
Form validation errors must be shown inline, next to the relevant field — not in a generic alert.
The application must work offline in read-only mode (service worker caching recent leads). Status changes must fail gracefully when offline.
12.4 Audit & Compliance
Every status change is permanently logged in lead_activity_log. Logs must never be deletable by any role including Admin.
Every document upload is logged with uploader, timestamp, and file hash (SHA-256).
Admin can export a full audit trail for any lead as PDF.
Data retention: leads and logs must be retained for minimum 7 years.
